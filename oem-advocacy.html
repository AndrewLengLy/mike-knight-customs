SEC / 01

The 100% OEM Structural Integrity Protocol

Parts Standard

Why It Matters

Your car was engineered once. We don't re-engineer it with cheaper metal.

Modern vehicles are single, integrated safety systems. Every panel thickness, weld point, and bracket position was validated in factory crash testing. Aftermarket and salvage parts, the LKQ and QRP line items adjusters quietly write into estimates, were not part of that test. At MKC, they are never part of the repair.

Ask About Your Estimate →
R/01

Compromised Crumple Zones

Crumple zones are engineered to collapse in a precise sequence, absorbing crash energy before it reaches the cabin. Aftermarket structural parts routinely vary in alloy, gauge, and geometry: small differences that change how force travels through the body in the next collision. A part that's 90% right is 100% untested.

R/02

Blinded ADAS Systems

Automatic emergency braking, lane keeping, and adaptive cruise depend on radar and camera units mounted to millimeter-accurate datum points in bumpers, grilles, and windshields. Off-spec brackets and imitation covers can skew sensor aim or attenuate radar. The system may show no warning light while seeing the road wrong. Every MKC structural repair concludes with OEM-procedure ADAS recalibration.

R/03

Eroded Resale & Diminished Value

A vehicle history showing non-OEM structural repair invites lower appraisals, failed pre-purchase inspections, and diminished value claims. Documented factory-part repair is the difference between a car that sells and a car that gets negotiated down.

SEC / 02

The Insurance Claims Advocacy Framework

Claims Procedure

From first call to final delivery, MKC operates as your protective shield. You hand us the claim number; we handle everything that follows. Here is exactly how.

01 Intake

We Take the Paperwork Off Your Desk

Claim filing, damage documentation, teardown photography, and the full repair plan, assembled by us, submitted by us. You get a single point of contact and plain-language updates. The adjuster gets a professionally documented file that's difficult to dispute.

You Provide

Claim number, keys, and a signature. That's the entire list.

02 OEM Advocacy

We Write the Repair to Factory Procedure

Every line of our estimate is tied to the manufacturer's published repair procedures: OEM part numbers, required welds, corrosion protection steps, and mandatory ADAS calibrations. When the estimate is anchored to the factory manual, "optional" stops being an argument the insurer can make.

Standard

OEM procedures only. No LKQ, no QRP, no "quality equivalent" substitutions.

03 Negotiation

We Push Back on Adjusters, So You Never Have To

When an adjuster tries to force imitation parts, cut labor hours, or skip refinish steps, that conversation happens with us, not with you. We supplement the claim with documented evidence, cite the factory procedure, and hold the line until the approved repair matches the required repair. Cheap parts don't get installed because they never get approved.

Our Position

The insurer owes you a proper repair, not the cheapest one they can close.

04 Refinish Mandate

We Mandate Premium Paint Blending

Insurers often approve paint for the damaged panel alone, a recipe for visible color mismatch. We require proper blending into adjacent panels, spectrophotometer-verified color formulation, and factory gloss standards, and we fight for the refinish hours that make an invisible transition possible.

Acceptance Test

If you can find the repair in direct sunlight, it isn't finished.

05 Delivery

You Get the Vehicle, and the Proof

Final delivery includes the complete repair file: OEM parts invoices, structural measurements, calibration reports, and refinish documentation. It protects your resale value, supports any diminished value claim, and backs the lifetime warranty we put on the work.

Warranty

Lifetime, for as long as you own the vehicle.
