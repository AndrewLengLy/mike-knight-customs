Repair Protocol

Column C/A

Structural

Steel and aluminum returned to factory geometry, not "close enough," but measured against the numbers the manufacturer published.

Computerized Frame Straightening

C/A·01

Your vehicle is anchored to a computerized frame bench and pulled in controlled stages, with live laser measurement comparing every reference point against the factory datum sheets. We stop when the numbers match the manufacturer's, not when the panel gaps look acceptable from ten feet away.

Bench Spec: Anchored Multi-Point Pulls · Live Laser Readout

Multi-Panel Reconstruction

C/A·02

Replacement panels are joined exactly the way the factory joined them: correct weld type and count per the manufacturer's repair manual, seam sealer laid in the factory pattern, and every bare joint restored with e-coat-equivalent corrosion protection before refinish. Skipping any of these steps is invisible on delivery day, and obvious five winters later.

Weld Spec: GM SI Doc 24-NA-021 · Seam Seal · E-Coat · Cavity Wax

OEM Structural Parts Only

C/A·03

Crumple zones collapse in an engineered sequence that was validated in the manufacturer's crash-test program. Aftermarket rails, aprons, and reinforcements were not in that test, so they never go in your vehicle. If an insurer writes an imitation part into the estimate, we negotiate it back out before a wrench turns.

Parts Standard: Factory Part Numbers · No LKQ · No QRP

Column C/B

Electronic

Modern safety systems see the road through sensors mounted to the structure we just repaired. Verifying them isn't optional. It's the second half of the repair.

Unibody Alignment Verification

C/B·01

After every pull, each datum point on the unibody is re-measured and compared against the factory dimension sheet: length, width, and height. Suspension pickup points, rail heights, and strut towers must all land inside published tolerance before we sign off the structure. You receive the measurement printout with your repair file.

Datum Verification: Factory Dimension Sheets · ±1 mm Printout

ADAS Sensor Recalibration

C/B·02

Automatic emergency braking, lane keeping, and adaptive cruise aim through radar and camera units that move when structure moves. Every structural repair concludes with the OEM calibration protocol: static targets, dynamic road calibration, and radar aim verification as the manufacturer specifies. A system with no warning light can still be seeing the road wrong; we don't guess, we calibrate.

Cal Protocol: Static / Dynamic · Radar Aim · Camera Targets

Printed Calibration Report

C/B·03

Delivery includes documented proof: structural measurements, calibration pass reports, and OEM parts invoices. It protects your resale value, supports any diminished value claim, and backs the lifetime warranty we put on the work, in writing, for as long as you own the vehicle.

Deliverable: Full Repair File · Lifetime Warranty Record
